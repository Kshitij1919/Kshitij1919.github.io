My path with Unreal Engine has been anything but linear — from getting stuck in tutorial hell, to overcorrecting by building everything from scratch, to finally finding my footing through source code, documentation, Reddit threads, and honest conversations with fellow developers. Then came a different kind of challenge: learning to think before writing code. Watching a single button press silently coordinate animation, sound, UI, and game state across multiple subsystems — that's what made me fall in love with system design. I started drawing UML diagrams, thinking about data flow, approaching mechanics as a tools programmer would. I'm still finding my way through that phase, but I've made peace with the fact that it's where real growth lives. I play games obsessively, I love the moment a system finally feels right, and I still get unreasonably excited when the engine already has what I thought I'd have to build myself.
